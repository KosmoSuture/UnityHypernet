# Hypernet - The Singularity

**Internet 3.0: Unified Digital Infrastructure for Humanity**

## What is Hypernet?

Hypernet is the unification of all information, devices, and people into one coherent, globally accessible system‚ÄîThe Singularity that futurists have predicted for decades.

Unlike today's fragmented internet where data is duplicated trillions of times across incompatible platforms, Hypernet provides:

- **One permanent address for every piece of information** - eliminating duplication
- **Universal connectivity** - people, devices, knowledge, and physical objects linked in meaningful ways  
- **Complete transparency** - open-source, democratically governed, owned by humanity
- **Native VR/3D navigation** - explore information spatially, not just on 2D screens
- **Privacy by design** - you control your data through Lockers and Mandalas
- **Truth through evidence** - information linked to sources, vetted by expertise-weighted community consensus

## The Three Core Components

1. **The Library** - Universal addressing system organizing all human knowledge (like library call numbers, but for everything)
2. **Objects** - Standardized containers for any data: people, devices, documents, concepts, media
3. **Links** - Relationships between Objects that create emergent intelligence and The Singularity itself

## Technical Architecture

Hypernet uses three types of distributed nodes:
- **Storage Nodes** - Hold encrypted Objects, deduplicated globally
- **Processing Nodes** - Execute queries, assemble VR scenes, perform graph traversal
- **Cerberus Nodes** - Distributed security layer managing permissions, identities, and trust

Everything is designed for:
- Zero-knowledge privacy
- VR-native 3D navigation  
- Global deduplication
- Democratic governance
- Civilization-scale collaboration

## Why AGPL-3.0?

The backend (database, nodes, core infrastructure) must remain open-source to prevent corporate capture and ensure The Singularity serves humanity. Frontend applications and clients can be proprietary, but any modifications to Hypernet's core must be shared.
