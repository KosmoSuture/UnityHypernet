## Purpose

This folder stores information about professional relationships, business contacts, colleagues, mentors, clients, and industry connections.

---

## What Goes Here

### Professional Relationship Types
- **Current Colleagues:** People you work with now
- **Former Colleagues:** Previous coworkers and teammates
- **Managers & Supervisors:** Current and past managers
- **Direct Reports:** People who report to you
- **Mentors:** Professional mentors and advisors
- **Mentees:** People you mentor
- **Clients & Customers:** Business clients and customer contacts
- **Vendors & Suppliers:** Service providers and suppliers
- **Business Partners:** Partnership and collaboration contacts
- **Industry Contacts:** Peers in your industry/field
- **Conference Connections:** People met at events
- **LinkedIn Connections:** Professional network contacts
- **Professional Association Members:** Industry group contacts
- **Alumni Networks:** School and program alumni

### Contact Information Stored
- Full name and preferred name
- Job title and company
- Contact details (email, phone, LinkedIn)
- How you know them / connection context
- When/where you met
- Relationship strength (weak/moderate/strong)
- Areas of expertise
- Mutual connections
- Communication history
- Last contact date
- Notes and context about relationship

## Privacy Considerations

### Default Privacy Level
- **Private:** Contact information is private
- **Professional:** Can reference relationships professionally
- **Restricted:** Personal notes about people stay private
- **Network Effect:** Respect others' privacy in your notes

### Data Sensitivity
- **Confidential:**
  - Personal mobile numbers
  - Home addresses
  - Private communication
  - Candid assessments of people
  - Salary/compensation information
  - Job search status
  - Personal life details

### Ethical Considerations
- Don't share others' contact info without permission
- Keep personal information secure
- Update when people change roles
- Respect preferences for contact methods
- Honor unsubscribe requests
- Be honest in relationship notes
- Maintain confidentiality

## Relationship Management

### Networking Best Practices
1. **Add Context Immediately:** Note how you met while fresh
2. **Follow Up Promptly:** Reach out within 48 hours of meeting
3. **Add Value:** Share relevant articles, make introductions
4. **Stay In Touch:** Don't only reach out when you need something
5. **Track Interactions:** Log important conversations
6. **Categorize Connections:** Tag by industry, skill, relationship type
7. **Nurture Relationships:** Regular check-ins with key contacts
8. **Give Before Ask:** Help others before asking for help
9. **Respect Boundaries:** Don't over-communicate
10. **Be Genuine:** Build authentic relationships

### Relationship Strength Indicators
- **Strong:**
  - Regular communication
  - Mutual trust and respect
  - Would recommend each other
  - Help each other proactively

- **Moderate:**
  - Occasional communication
  - Professional respect
  - Would respond to requests
  - Share information when relevant

- **Weak:**
  - Rare communication
  - Acquaintance level
  - May not remember well
  - Need to reintroduce yourself

## Communication Tracking

### Track Interactions
- **Date:** When communication occurred
- **Method:** Email, call, coffee, conference, etc.
- **Topic:** What was discussed
- **Outcome:** Decisions, commitments, next steps
- **Follow-up:** What needs to happen next
- **Notes:** Important details to remember

### Stay In Touch Schedule
- **Key Contacts:** Monthly or quarterly check-ins
- **Active Relationships:** Every 2-3 months
- **Moderate Connections:** Every 6 months
- **Weak Connections:** Annually or as needed
- **Dormant Connections:** Reach out when relevant

7. **Give Back:** Help your network, don't just take
