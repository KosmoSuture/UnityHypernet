## Purpose

This folder stores all personal legal documents including wills, trusts, powers of attorney, court documents, and other legally significant personal papers.

## Privacy Considerations

### Default Privacy Level
- **MAXIMUM PRIVACY:** Highest security for legal documents
- **Strictly Private:** Only Matt has access
- **Legacy Access:** Executor/attorney access after death
- **Never Public:** These documents should never be public

### Data Sensitivity
- **Extremely Sensitive:**
  - Contains wealth and asset information
  - Personal health directives
  - Family arrangements and disputes
  - Legal strategies and positions
  - Beneficiary information
  - Executor and trustee designations

### Security Measures
- **Required:**
  - Strong encryption at rest and in transit
  - Multi-factor authentication
  - Secure backup in multiple locations
  - Physical copies in safe deposit box
  - Attorney copy maintained
  - Clear chain of custody

- **Prohibited:**
  - Cloud storage without encryption
  - Email transmission without encryption
  - Sharing via unsecured methods
  - AI training data usage

### Access Control
- **Primary Access:** Matt only
- **Emergency Access:** Designated person with sealed instructions
- **Attorney Access:** Specific attorney can access relevant documents
- **Executor Access:** After death, per estate plan
- **No AI Access:** Legal documents excluded from AI analysis

## Document Maintenance

### Keep Current
- **Review Annually:** Review all estate planning documents
- **Update After Life Changes:**
  - Marriage or divorce
  - Birth or adoption of children
  - Significant asset changes
  - Move to new state
  - Death of beneficiary or executor
  - Change in relationships

- **Attorney Review:**
  - Every 3-5 years minimum
  - After major life events
  - When laws change
  - When moving to new state

### Version Control
- Always keep superseded versions
- Note date and reason for changes
- Document who prepared updates
- Track execution dates
- Maintain amendment history

## Critical Information to Document

### For Estate Planning
- **Executor/Trustee:** Name, contact, alternates
- **Beneficiaries:** Full names, relationships, percentages
- **Asset Inventory:** What you own and where it is
- **Account Information:** Banks, investments, insurance
- **Digital Assets:** Passwords, accounts, crypto wallets
- **Debts:** What you owe and to whom
- **Advisors:** Attorney, accountant, financial advisor contacts

### For Healthcare Directives
- **Healthcare Agent:** Name, contact, alternates
- **Medical Wishes:** Life support preferences
- **Organ Donation:** Yes/no and specifics
- **Physician Information:** Primary doctors and specialists
- **Allergies and Conditions:** Critical medical information

## Best Practices

1. **Original + Copies:** Keep original plus secure copies
2. **Physical + Digital:** Both formats recommended
3. **Multiple Locations:** Don't keep all in one place
4. **Inform Trusted Person:** Someone should know where to find documents
5. **Professional Preparation:** Use qualified attorney for critical documents
6. **Proper Execution:** Ensure documents are properly signed and witnessed
7. **State-Specific:** Follow state law requirements
8. **Regular Updates:** Review and update periodically
9. **Secure Storage:** Use safe deposit box and/or fireproof safe
10. **Emergency Access:** Ensure documents accessible in emergency
