## Purpose

This folder stores formal letters, professional correspondence, and important written communications that are distinct from email and meeting notes.

---

## What Goes Here

### Correspondence Types
- **Formal Letters:** Business letters, personal formal letters
- **Professional Communication:** Cover letters, recommendation letters
- **Thank You Notes:** Professional gratitude communications
- **Reference Letters:** Letters of recommendation (written or received)
- **Complaint Letters:** Formal complaints and dispute correspondence
- **Inquiry Letters:** Information requests, inquiries
- **Notification Letters:** Important notifications and announcements
- **Resignation Letters:** Job resignation letters
- **Offer Letters:** Job offers and acceptance letters
- **Acknowledgment Letters:** Confirmations and acknowledgments
- **Appreciation Letters:** Recognition and appreciation
- **Introduction Letters:** Professional introductions

### Physical vs Digital
- **Physical Letters (Scanned):** Handwritten or typed letters received by mail
- **Digital Letters:** Formal letters created digitally
- **Email Attachments:** Formal letters sent as email attachments
- **Postal Mail:** Scanned copies of mailed correspondence

## Privacy Considerations

### Default Privacy Level
- **Private:** Personal correspondence is private
- **Professional:** Business correspondence may be shareable
- **Confidential:** Some letters contain sensitive information
- **Public:** Reference letters may be shared with permission

### Data Sensitivity
- **High Sensitivity:**
  - Resignation letters (compensation details)
  - Offer letters (salary information)
  - Recommendation letters (candid assessments)
  - Personal letters (intimate communications)
  - Complaint letters (disputes and conflicts)

- **Medium Sensitivity:**
  - Thank you notes
  - Professional introductions
  - General business letters
  - Inquiries and requests

- **Low Sensitivity:**
  - Public acknowledgments
  - General announcements
  - Non-confidential business letters

## Letter Writing Best Practices

### Professional Letter Format
```
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[City, State ZIP]
[Email]
[Phone]

[Date]

[Recipient Name]
[Recipient Title]
[Company/Organization]
[Address]
[City, State ZIP]

Dear [Recipient Name],

[Opening paragraph: State purpose]

[Body paragraphs: Provide details, context, reasoning]

[Closing paragraph: Call to action, next steps]

Sincerely,

[Your Signature]
[Your Typed Name]
```

### Essential Elements
- Professional letterhead (if applicable)
- Clear subject line or reference
- Proper salutation
- Concise and focused content
- Professional tone
- Clear call to action
- Proper closing
- Contact information

## Types of Letters - Details

### Resignation Letter
**Purpose:** Formally notify employer of intent to leave
**Include:**
- Resignation date
- Last day of work
- Reason (optional, keep brief)
- Gratitude for opportunity
- Offer to help with transition
- Professional tone

### Thank You Letter
**Purpose:** Express gratitude
**Include:**
- Specific reason for thanks
- Impact of their action/gift
- Personal touch
- Forward-looking statement

### Recommendation Letter
**Purpose:** Endorse someone's abilities
**Include:**
- Your relationship to person
- Their qualifications and strengths
- Specific examples and achievements
- Strong endorsement
- Your credentials/expertise

### Cover Letter
**Purpose:** Accompany job application
**Include:**
- Position applying for
- Why you're interested
- Relevant qualifications
- What you bring to role
- Call to action (interview request)

## Best Practices

1. **Save All Drafts:** Keep versions of important letters
2. **Date Everything:** Always include date on letters
3. **Professional Format:** Use proper business letter format
4. **Proofread Carefully:** No typos in formal correspondence
5. **Keep Copies:** Save copies of all sent letters
6. **Digital + Physical:** Scan physical letters, save digital as PDF
7. **Organize by Type:** Group similar correspondence together
8. **Context Notes:** Add notes about why letter was sent
9. **Track Responses:** Note if/when response received
10. **Template Library:** Save templates for common letter types

## Retention Guidelines

### Keep Permanently
- Job offer letters
- Resignation letters
- Important recommendation letters
- Award or recognition letters
- Significant personal letters

### Keep 7+ Years
- Business correspondence
- Formal complaints and resolutions
- Tax-related correspondence
- Legal correspondence

### Keep 3-5 Years
- General business letters
- Professional thank you notes
- Routine correspondence

### Can Delete After 1 Year
- Routine thank you notes
- General inquiries
- Non-critical correspondence

---

## Special Considerations

### Recommendation Letters
- Keep copies of all recommendations you write
- Save recommendations received for you
- Update reference letters periodically
- Ask permission before using as reference
- Redact sensitive info if sharing

### Resignation Letters
- Never burn bridges
- Keep professional and positive
- Provide adequate notice
- Offer to help with transition
- Keep copy for your records

### Legal Correspondence
- Keep all legal letters permanently
- Don't alter or delete
- May be evidence in disputes
- Consult attorney about retention
- Maintain chain of custody
