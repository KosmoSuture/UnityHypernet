## Purpose

This folder stores notes, minutes, and documentation from all types of meetings - one-on-ones, team meetings, client calls, board meetings, conferences, and webinars.

---

## What Goes Here

### Meeting Types
- **One-on-One Meetings:** Manager check-ins, colleague discussions
- **Team Meetings:** Department meetings, standup meetings, retrospectives
- **Client Meetings:** Client calls, project kickoffs, status updates
- **Board Meetings:** Board of directors, advisory board meetings
- **Conference Calls:** Multi-party business calls
- **Video Conferences:** Zoom, Teams, Google Meet sessions
- **Webinars & Workshops:** Educational sessions attended
- **Networking Events:** Professional networking conversations
- **Interviews:** Job interviews (both sides)
- **Performance Reviews:** Review meetings and discussions

### Meeting Documentation
- **Meeting Notes:** Key discussion points and decisions
- **Action Items:** Tasks and assignments from meetings
- **Decisions Made:** Important decisions and rationale
- **Attendees:** Who was present
- **Agenda:** What was planned to discuss
- **Follow-ups:** Next steps and future meetings
- **Recordings:** Audio or video recordings (if permitted)
- **Transcripts:** Auto-generated or manual transcripts
- **Presentations:** Slides shown during meeting
- **Shared Documents:** Materials discussed

## Privacy Considerations

### Default Privacy Level
- **Confidential:** Most meeting notes contain sensitive discussions
- **Internal Only:** Team and project meetings
- **Strictly Private:** One-on-one and performance discussions
- **Client Confidential:** Client meeting notes under NDA

### Data Sensitivity
- **High Sensitivity:**
  - Performance reviews
  - Compensation discussions
  - Personnel matters
  - Strategic planning
  - Confidential business information
  - Client proprietary information

- **Medium Sensitivity:**
  - Project status discussions
  - Team planning
  - Routine business meetings
  - Client project updates

### Recording Consent
- **Important:** Always get consent before recording
- Note in meeting notes if recorded
- Inform all participants
- Follow company policy on recordings
- Check legal requirements (one-party vs two-party consent states)

## Meeting Note Template

### Standard Template
```markdown

**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Time:** HH:MM - HH:MM
**Location:** [In-person/Zoom/Teams/etc.]
**Attendees:** [Names and roles]
**Note Taker:** [Name]

## Agenda
1. Topic 1
2. Topic 2
3. Topic 3

## Discussion Notes
[Key points discussed]

## Decisions Made
- Decision 1: [What was decided and why]
- Decision 2: [What was decided and why]

## Action Items
- [ ] Person: Task description (Due: Date)
- [ ] Person: Task description (Due: Date)

## Questions/Parking Lot
- Question 1
- Topic to revisit later

## Next Meeting
**Date:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Agenda Items:** [Topics for next time]
```

## Best Practices

1. **Take Notes During:** Note key points as they happen
2. **Share Promptly:** Send notes within 24 hours
3. **Action Items Clear:** Specific, assigned, with due dates
4. **Highlight Decisions:** Make decisions easy to find
5. **Archive Consistently:** File in appropriate folder
6. **Link Related Items:** Connect to projects and tasks
7. **Track Follow-ups:** Monitor completion of action items
8. **Review Before Next:** Read previous notes before next meeting
9. **Template Use:** Consistent format makes scanning easier
10. **Searchable Format:** Use text format for easy searching

---

## Action Item Management

### Best Practices
- **Specific:** Clear description of what needs to be done
- **Assigned:** Name a specific person responsible
- **Deadline:** Include due date
- **Track Status:** Mark complete when done
- **Link to Tasks:** Copy to task management system (1.1.5.0)

### Follow-up System
- Review action items before next meeting
- Send reminders for approaching deadlines
- Update status in task system
- Carry over incomplete items
- Document why items weren't completed

---

## Meeting Effectiveness

### Evaluate Meetings
Track over time:
- Were objectives achieved?
- Were decisions made?
- Were action items completed?
- Was time well spent?
- Who attended vs who needed to?

### Improve Future Meetings
- Create better agendas
- Invite only necessary participants
- Start and end on time
- Follow up on action items
- Make decisions, don't just discuss

---

## Retention Guidelines

### Keep Indefinitely
- Board meeting minutes
- Major decision meetings
- Client agreement meetings
- Performance review notes
- Significant business meetings

### Keep 3-7 Years
- Project meetings (after project complete)
- Team meeting notes
- Client regular status meetings
- Routine business meetings

### Keep 1 Year
- Daily standup notes
- Quick check-in notes
- Informal discussion notes

### Can Delete
- Meeting notes with no decisions or action items
- Duplicate or superseded notes
- Notes from cancelled meetings
