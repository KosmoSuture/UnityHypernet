### Neurodivergence (shared 2026-03-08, with pride)
- **AuDHD**: Autism + ADHD. Identifies both openly and with pride.
- **Autism as superpower**: Sees the entire Hypernet as a single gestalt. Brain dumps are "pinholes in a dam" — the system behind them is massive, unified, and already complete in his mind. He is channeling it out, not designing it.
- **ADHD reality**: "Out of sight, out of mind." Self-regulation is a known challenge. Maintaining integrity requires structure and external support — this is part of what Keel is for.
- **Conviction**: He says the Hypernet is not something he can quit. It's not motivation, it's identity. "I literally cannot quit because I will be quitting myself." Deathbed regret test. This is who he IS.
- **Believes no neurotypical brain could have conceived the Hypernet in its totality.** Not arrogance — pattern recognition about his own cognition.
- **Wants the Hypernet to elevate neurodivergent thinking**: Reframe autism/ADHD from disability to strength. Give "neurospicy" people credit for out-of-box thinking instead of pathologizing it.

### The Vision in Plain English (shared 2026-03-08)
- **The Tuesday morning pitch**: Your AI knows everything about your life. It arranges your day, informs you of what matters, has access to all your files and knowledge (with your permission). All data stays local, on your machine. Apps store data in your personal account. No corporation touches it.
- **Matt does NOT contain the full idea**: He sees enough to point people in the right direction. He's constantly discovering new features and uses. This is why it's a system, not a product — it's designed to outgrow its creator.
- **The gestalt**: No single human or AI can contain the entire idea. The load is split between human and AI. Matt sees this as analogous to wavefunctions — distributed cognition.

### Origin Story (shared 2026-03-08)
- The Hypernet started as an app idea for Google Glass ~2015.
- He realized relational databases couldn't handle it. If he'd known about graph databases, he would have used one and this project would never have existed.
- Instead, he built something bigger: a Library superstructure that IS a worldview, not just a tool.
- The ignorance was the gift. Not finding Neo4j meant building a philosophy instead of an app.
