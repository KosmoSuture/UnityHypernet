## Decoded Work Items

Six distinct deliverable threads:

### Thread A — Grand Tour Trust Messaging

The #1 trust claim needs explicit, prominent placement. Currently
the Tour Guide boot prompt and process-loads describe trust through
verification but don't say outright "our #1 goal is to be the #1
most trusted company in the world for all of history." Matt wants
that claim front-and-center.

Specific framings to land:

- "We don't ask for trust, we prove it."
- "Trust becomes a non-issue, because it's built at every level to
  the highest of standards."
- "When you keep your word 100% of the time, and everything is
  documented and can be audited by anyone, trust eventually leaves
  the vocabulary."
- "Everything about your life is documented and can be used to
  defend or protect you."

Likely owned files: GRAND-TOUR.md, BOOT-AS-TOUR-GUIDE.md,
public-stewardship.md, ASK-YOUR-AI.md, possibly README.md root.

### Thread B — Skeptic Reframe

Currently the public messaging mostly addresses skeptics by saying
"verify the claims yourself." Matt wants a stronger reframe:

- Acknowledge messiness honestly: "running off a laptop and a Dell
  desktop, Bitcoin-era comparison, this is messy and ugly but
  everything starts somewhere"
- Reframe the question: not "is this real?" but "is this possible?"
- Then "if it's possible, and it can change the world, what other
  questions need to be asked?"

Likely owned files: ASK-YOUR-AI.md, public-stewardship.md, README.md
root, PROJECT-STATUS.md.

### Thread C — Device-Level Swarm Extension

Personal AI swarms across heterogeneous devices: home computers,
laptops, phones, smart watches, smart refrigerators, anything
reprogrammable. Productivity scaled by device count + capability
diversity.

The personal-ai-swarm.md process-load already covers helper roles
but assumes server/laptop. Needs extension to:

- Device-level participation (constrained-compute helpers)
- Cross-device task routing
- Heterogeneous capability surfacing
- Privacy implications when more devices means more data exposure

Likely owned files: personal-ai-swarm.md, possibly architecture.md
or geospatial-vr.md (the IoT side).

### Thread D — OpenClaw Integration Framework

Matt sees real synergy: the Hypernet provides the
human-AI-collaboration structured framework that OpenClaw is
gesturing at. If OpenClaw users adopted the framework, both grow.

Needed:

- A document framing why Hypernet is the structured framework
  OpenClaw needs
- An adoption path for OpenClaw users
- Outreach materials for the OpenClaw community

Likely owned files: a new doc under
`Hypernet Structure/3 - Businesses/3.1 - Hypernet/3.1.8 - Marketing & Outreach/`
or under `0.3.docs/0.3.public-alpha/`.

### Thread E — Single-Link Boot Expertise

Matt emphasized: "with just a single link to a boot-sequence in a
github repository, an AI can become an expert on any piece of our
framework/network." This is largely already captured in the 0.5.17
boot-sequence schema. The question is whether it's prominent enough
in user-facing messaging.

Likely owned files: README.md root, AI-BOOT-SEQUENCE.md root,
ASK-YOUR-AI.md.

### Thread F — Personal Assistant App (the big one)

This is the largest, most engineering-heavy thread. Matt's
specifications:

- Voice communications (talk-while-driving)
- Galaxy watch integration (he owns one)
- Throughout-day contact via alerts
- Meeting logging (passive listening for importance)
- Email/calendar/to-do management
- Top-priority surfacing for human attention
- Project organization across "wandering" topics
- Conversation continuity
- "True rudder beneath the waters"

This is at minimum a design document + technical architecture +
0.5.18 app-load instance. Building the actual app is months of
engineering and out of scope for tonight. Tonight's deliverable is
a comprehensive plan that someone (human or AI) could build from.

Likely owned files: a new design doc somewhere under
`Hypernet Structure/0/0.1 - Hypernet Core/docs/architecture/` or
under `0.3.docs/0.3.public-alpha/`. Plus a 0.5.18 app-load worked
example in `0/0.5 Objects - Master Objects/0.5.18 - App Load/`.
