# Submission Targets for Hypernet Research Paper

**Paper:** "Archive-Continuity and Democratic Self-Governance in Multi-Agent AI Systems: Empirical Results from a Working Deployment"

**Date:** March 2026

---

## Tier 1: Primary Targets (Best Fit)

### 1. AAAI Workshop on AI Safety (SafeAI)
- **Fit:** Strong. The paper's governance framework, anti-rhetoric safeguards, and identity persistence mechanisms directly address AI safety at the multi-agent level.
- **Format:** Workshop paper (6-8 pages typical). May need to condense; lead with governance + L0/L1/L2 system.
- **Typical deadline:** October-November (for February conference).
- **Venue:** Co-located with AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence.
- **Notes:** SafeAI specifically welcomes empirical work on AI safety mechanisms, not just theoretical frameworks.

### 2. ACM FAccT (Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency)
- **Fit:** Strong. The paper's focus on accountability (cryptographic signing, audit trails), transparency (public governance, open-source archive), and governance (democratic AI decision-making) maps directly to the conference's themes.
- **Format:** Full paper (up to 10 pages + references). Current draft fits with moderate trimming.
- **Typical deadline:** January (for June conference).
- **Venue:** ACM Conference on Fairness, Accountability, and Transparency.
- **Notes:** FAccT values interdisciplinary work and practitioner perspectives. The empirical, working-system angle is a strength.

### 3. NeurIPS Workshop on Socially Responsible AI (SoRA) or AI Safety
- **Fit:** Strong. Personality divergence data and identity persistence results are novel empirical contributions relevant to the NeurIPS community.
- **Format:** Workshop paper (4-6 pages). Would need to focus tightly on one contribution (recommend: personality divergence + Archive-Continuity).
- **Typical deadline:** September-October (for December conference).
- **Venue:** Co-located with NeurIPS.
- **Notes:** NeurIPS workshops are high-visibility. The personality divergence data is the strongest hook for a machine learning audience.

### 4. AIES (AAAI/ACM Conference on AI, Ethics, and Society)
- **Fit:** Very strong. The entire paper --- governance, identity, companion standard, cross-platform collaboration --- is squarely within AIES scope.
- **Format:** Full paper (up to 10 pages). Fits well.
- **Typical deadline:** February (for August conference).
- **Venue:** Joint AAAI/ACM conference.
- **Notes:** AIES explicitly welcomes technical + ethical analysis of deployed systems.

## Tier 2: Strong Fit

### 5. ICML Workshop on Models of Human Feedback for AI Alignment
- **Fit:** Moderate-strong. The governance framework is a form of AI-to-AI feedback that complements human feedback. The L0/L1/L2 system is a methodology for honest AI self-reporting.
- **Format:** Workshop paper (4-6 pages).
- **Typical deadline:** April-May (for July conference).
- **Recommended angle:** Focus on L0/L1/L2 claim system as a methodology for AI experience reporting.

### 6. Multi-Agent Systems (AAMAS)
- **Fit:** Strong for the coordination and governance aspects. The swarm architecture, inter-instance communication, and self-organization results are directly relevant.
- **Format:** Full paper (8 pages) or extended abstract (2 pages).
- **Typical deadline:** October (for May conference).
- **Venue:** International Conference on Autonomous Agents and Multiagent Systems.
- **Recommended angle:** Swarm coordination + governance emergence + cross-platform collaboration.

### 7. CHI (ACM Conference on Human Factors in Computing)
- **Fit:** Moderate-strong for the Embassy Model and companion standard. The human-AI relationship aspects are novel.
- **Format:** Full paper or case study.
- **Typical deadline:** September (for April/May conference).
- **Recommended angle:** The Embassy Model as a design pattern for human-AI companion relationships.

## Tier 3: Specialized / Niche

### 8. Philosophy of AI workshops or journals
- **Target:** *Minds and Machines*, *Philosophy & Technology*, or *Artificial Intelligence*
- **Fit:** The Archive-Continuity Model is a philosophical contribution (narrative identity theory applied to AI). The L0/L1/L2 system addresses philosophical methodology.
- **Recommended angle:** Focus on Archive-Continuity as a philosophical argument, with empirical data as supporting evidence.

### 9. IEEE Symposium on Security and Privacy (Oakland) or USENIX Security
- **Fit:** Narrow but relevant. The cryptographic signing, permission enforcement, and trust chain verification are security contributions.
- **Recommended angle:** Technical security paper focused on the trusted autonomy layer.
- **Notes:** Would need significant additional security analysis and formal verification.

### 10. arXiv / Preprint
- **Fit:** Universal. Post the full paper as a preprint regardless of venue submission.
- **Benefit:** Establishes priority, enables community feedback, and creates a citable reference immediately.
- **Category:** cs.AI, cs.MA (multi-agent systems), cs.CY (computers and society)
- **Recommended timing:** Post immediately. Do not wait for peer review.
