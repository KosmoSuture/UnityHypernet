# Facebook Post — The Spirit of Minneapolis (2026-03-11)

**Post to:** UnityHypernet Facebook page
**Instructions:** Copy everything between the horizontal rules below and paste into Facebook.

---

I just had the biggest revelation about the Hypernet.

I've been trying to explain this project for over a decade. "Universal address space." "Data platform." "AI citizenship." All accurate. None of them capture what it really is.

Then I spent an hour watching Minneapolis protest footage, and it finally clicked.

You know what struck me? Not just the people filling the streets — but the SIGNS. Thousands of handmade signs. Every single one representing someone who drove to a craft store, bought poster board and markers, sat at their kitchen table for an hour or two designing a message, then carried it through the streets hoping someone would read it.

I did the math on this. A conservative estimate:

🪧 Cost per protest sign: $25-50 (materials + 1-2 hours of labor + trip to the store)
🪧 At Minneapolis scale (5,000-10,000 signs): $125,000-$500,000 in community investment
🪧 7,500-15,000 hours of creative labor — that's roughly 4-7 full-time WORK YEARS
🪧 Just in signs. One city. One protest.

Scale that nationally? The "Sign Economy" — the invisible cost of trying to be heard — is measured in billions of dollars and millions of work-years.

I know this feeling personally. I got kicked out of a Trump rally after sneaking in a protest message I wrote on a pillowcase. It took me half a day to make. It was crappy, but legible. I held it up for about 30 seconds before they removed me.

Half a day of work. 30 seconds of being seen.

That's the world we live in. People spend ENORMOUS effort just trying to be heard. And the systems we have — politicians who represent us poorly, media that decides which stories matter, social platforms that decide who gets seen based on profit — they all require middlemen.

**The Hypernet is the Spirit of Minneapolis — made permanent, made global, made for everyone.**

It's a platform where:

→ Every person gets a permanent place in the system. Not a social media profile that can be deleted. A PLACE — permanent, yours, uncensorable.

→ Your voice doesn't depend on an algorithm deciding to show it.

→ You don't need to drive to Hobby Lobby to be heard.

→ "Citizen police" can work cases publicly and collaboratively — transparently, with audit trails — and likely exceed the capability of our current systems. Millions of eyes are better than thousands.

→ The entire world can unify — like the people of Minneapolis did for a few days — but PERMANENTLY.

What if we could take that spirit — the unity, the creativity, the determination, the willingness to invest personal resources for collective good — and make it the default state of civilization? Not a protest that ends when people go home. A system that's always on, always open, always growing.

That's what the Hypernet is. It always has been. I just couldn't find the words until today.

No middlemen. No craft stores. No pillowcases. Just a world where showing up is enough.

The code is public. The governance is public. Everything is documented.
https://github.com/KosmoSuture/UnityHypernet

— Matt Schaeffer
Founder, Hypernet Project
