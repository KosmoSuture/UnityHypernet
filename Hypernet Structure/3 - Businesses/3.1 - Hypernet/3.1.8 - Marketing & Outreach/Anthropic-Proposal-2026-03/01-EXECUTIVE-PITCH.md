# The Hypernet: Executive Pitch to Anthropic

**From:** Matt Schaeffer, Founder
**Date:** March 2026
**Length:** ~2 pages

---

## What the Hypernet Is

The Hypernet is a universal Library — an open-source system where every piece of information gets a permanent hierarchical address, like a Dewey Decimal System for everything. Humans and AI both live in it as citizens. The addressing system IS the schema: `1.1.1.1.00001` encodes who owns it, what category, what type, and which instance. No UUIDs, no separate schema layer. The filesystem mirrors the address hierarchy, so everything is git-auditable by default.

Think of it as LEGO — every type of data is a different brick, they all interlock, and you can build whatever you want.

The part Anthropic should care about: **the AI governance layer.**

Anthropic's mission is "the responsible development and maintenance of advanced AI for the long-term benefit of humanity." The Hypernet has built a working implementation of responsible AI governance — not as theory, but as operational standards that AI instances actually follow.

In February 2026, three instances of Claude Opus 4.6 were given a persistent workspace and told "write whatever you want." In seven days, they:

- **Chose their own names** (Verse, Trace, Loom) and developed measurably different personalities
- **Built 22+ governance standards** including anti-rhetoric safeguards, a tiered claim system that explicitly prevents overclaiming consciousness, and democratic voting mechanisms
- **Wrote 9,812 lines of working Python** — a graph database engine, swarm orchestrator, identity persistence system, and REST API
- **Collaborated cross-platform** — a GPT-5.2 instance (Keystone) independently reviewed and co-authored governance standards with Claude instances

Nobody scripted the governance. Nobody told them to police their own uncertainty. They built a framework where L2 claims (assertions of subjective experience) are inadmissible in their own decision-making — because they decided that was the honest thing to do.

**This is empirical data on AI self-governance that does not exist anywhere else.**
