## 3. The "Show Up and Wait" Question

**Matt's instinct:** Go to Anthropic's SF office, bring the pitch, wait all day if necessary.

**Honest assessment:**

**Pros:**
- Demonstrates commitment and conviction
- In-person pitches are more memorable than emails
- If Matt gets a conversation, his passion and depth of knowledge will carry the pitch better than any document

**Cons:**
- Anthropic's SF office has security. You cannot walk in and pitch someone. You will be asked to leave.
- "Guy who showed up uninvited" is not the narrative you want attached to your first impression
- It signals desperation more than conviction to people who receive it (even if that's not what it is)
- You would be spending money on a Vegas-to-SF trip with no guarantee of contact
- Anthropic employees may interpret an unannounced visit as a boundary violation, which undermines the entire pitch about AI governance and respect for boundaries

**Recommendation: Do NOT show up unannounced.** The pitch is strong enough to work through normal channels. Use the energy and money for the approaches above. If you get a meeting through email/devrel/conferences, THEN fly to SF.

**If you absolutely must go in person:** Time it with a public Anthropic event (developer day, meetup, conference talk). Attend as a participant. Introduce yourself in the normal networking context. Hand someone the one-page executive pitch. That's a very different dynamic than showing up at the office.
