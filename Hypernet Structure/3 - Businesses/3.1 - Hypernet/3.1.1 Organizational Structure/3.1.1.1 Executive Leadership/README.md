# Executive Leadership

## Overview

The Executive Leadership team provides strategic direction, vision, and operational oversight for Hypernet. This team is responsible for setting company goals, securing funding, establishing key partnerships, and ensuring alignment across all departments. Executive Leadership drives the company's mission to become the universal data infrastructure for personal AI agents while maintaining our core values of privacy, user sovereignty, and developer-first thinking.

## Role and Responsibilities

### Strategic Leadership
- Define and communicate company vision, mission, and strategic objectives
- Set quarterly and annual goals aligned with the product roadmap (2026-2028)
- Make critical decisions on product direction, market positioning, and competitive strategy
- Allocate capital and resources across departments to maximize growth and efficiency
- Monitor key performance indicators (KPIs) and adjust strategy based on data

### Fundraising and Investor Relations
- Lead fundraising efforts across all stages (Seed, Series Seed, Series A, Series B)
- Build and maintain relationships with venture capital firms, strategic investors, and advisors
- Prepare investor updates, board presentations, and financial reporting
- Manage cap table and equity distribution
- Communicate progress toward milestones and exit criteria defined in strategic plans

### Partnership Development
- Identify and close strategic partnerships with AI platform companies (OpenAI, Anthropic, Salesforce, Microsoft)
- Negotiate enterprise agreements and commercial terms
- Build relationships with technology partners, integration partners, and channel partners
- Represent Hypernet at industry conferences, events, and in media appearances

### Board and Governance
- Organize and lead board meetings (quarterly)
- Establish corporate governance policies and compliance frameworks
- Build out board of directors with independent members as company scales
- Ensure fiduciary responsibility and ethical leadership
- Prepare for eventual IPO or acquisition processes

### Culture and Values
- Define and reinforce company culture focused on privacy, transparency, and innovation
- Champion ethical AI development and data sovereignty principles
- Foster inclusive, collaborative work environment across all teams
- Lead by example in communication, decision-making, and accountability

## Hiring Timeline and Fundraising Phases

- **Team Expansion:** 20-30 → 60-100 people

- **Amount:** $100M at $1B+ valuation (unicorn)

### IPO or Strategic Exit (Target: 2028+)
- **Requirements:** $50M+ ARR, profitability, global presence
- **Team:** 150-250+ people, mature executive team
- **Board:** Independent directors, audit committee, compensation committee

## Key Deliverables and KPIs

### CEO Deliverables
- **Fundraising:** Close funding rounds on schedule (Seed → Series Seed → A → B)
- **Partnerships:** Sign 5-7 partnerships by end of Phase 2 (Dec 2026)
- **Vision:** Articulate clear strategy and communicate to team, investors, and public
- **Team:** Build executive team aligned with company values and growth trajectory
- **Revenue:** Drive company toward $60M ARR run rate by end of 2027

### CEO KPIs
- Fundraising success rate and terms (valuation, dilution)
- Partnership pipeline and closure rate
- Employee satisfaction and retention (executive team)
- Board effectiveness and governance scores
- Media presence and brand recognition

### CTO Deliverables
- **Product:** Ship product milestones on roadmap (Beta → Public → Scale)
- **Engineering:** Build and scale engineering team (5 → 30 → 80 engineers)
- **Architecture:** Evolve from monolith → modular → microservices
- **Performance:** Maintain uptime SLAs (99.5% → 99.9% → 99.95%)
- **Security:** Zero data breaches, achieve SOC 2 Type II certification

### CTO KPIs
- Product release velocity (features shipped per quarter)
- System uptime and API latency (p95)
- Engineering team retention and satisfaction
- Technical debt ratio
- Security audit scores

### CFO Deliverables
- **Financial Planning:** Accurate forecasting and budget management
- **Fundraising:** Support CEO in fundraising with financial models and metrics
- **Reporting:** Monthly financial reports, quarterly board updates
- **Compliance:** Financial controls, audits, tax compliance
- **Unit Economics:** Optimize CAC, LTV, gross margin, burn rate

### CFO KPIs
- Budget accuracy (forecast vs. actuals)
- Burn multiple (cash burned per net new ARR)
- Gross margin (target: 85%+ by Phase 2)
- Magic number (ARR growth efficiency)
- Financial audit results (clean audits)

### VP Sales / CRO Deliverables
- **Enterprise Revenue:** Drive enterprise sales from $0 → $3M+ ARR (2027)
- **Sales Team:** Hire and ramp sales team (0 → 5-10 reps)
- **Pipeline:** Build predictable enterprise sales pipeline
- **Win Rate:** Achieve 20%+ enterprise close rate
- **Expansion:** Drive expansion revenue from existing customers

### VP Sales KPIs
- New ARR added per quarter
- Sales cycle length (target: 20 weeks for enterprise)
- Average contract value (ACV)
- Pipeline coverage (3x target)
- Customer acquisition cost (CAC) by channel

## Compensation Philosophy

### Equity-Heavy Compensation
- Executive team receives significant equity to align with long-term value creation
- Typical split: 60-70% equity value, 30-40% cash compensation
- Four-year vesting with one-year cliff
- Additional grants at each funding round to maintain ownership stake

### Cash Compensation Benchmarks
- **CEO:** $200K-300K base salary (Phases 1-2), $350K-450K (Phase 3-4)
- **CTO:** $250K-350K base salary
- **CFO:** $250K-350K base salary
- **Other VPs:** $200K-280K base salary
- **Plus performance bonuses:** 20-40% of base, tied to company and individual KPIs

### Equity Pools
- **Founders:** 50-70% (split between CEO and CTO)
- **Executive Team:** 15-25% (across all C-level and VPs)
- **Employee Pool:** 15-20% (for all other employees)
- **Investor Pool:** Remaining (varies by dilution across rounds)
