## Executive Summary

**Goal:** Acquire developer hardware from 3-5 major VR manufacturers and establish partnerships to support universal VR compatibility in Hypernet.

**Timeline:** 8 weeks for initial campaign

**Budget:** $0-2,500 (ideally $0 through developer programs and partnerships)

**Success Metrics:**
- 8+ manufacturers contacted
- 50%+ response rate
- 3-5 developer kits acquired
- 1-2 formal partnerships

## The Value Proposition

Hypernet is building an **open-source, universal VR platform** that allows developers to build once and deploy to all VR headsets. This benefits manufacturers by:

1. **Expanding their developer ecosystem** (lower barriers = more content)
2. **Growing the total VR market** (rising tide lifts all boats)
3. **Maintaining competitive differentiation** (hardware, features, ecosystem)
4. **Supporting enterprise adoption** (cross-platform = enterprise requirement)
5. **Future-proofing** through open standards

We're asking for **developer hardware** (loan or donation) and optionally technical support. In return, manufacturers get native Hypernet support and recognition as supporters of open VR development.

When communicating with manufacturers, always highlight:

## Common Questions & Answers

### "Won't this commoditize our hardware?"
**No.** You still compete on:
- Hardware quality (display, tracking, comfort)
- Platform features (hand tracking, eye tracking, etc.)
- Exclusive content (if desired)
- Brand and ecosystem

**Analogy:** All PCs run Windows, but manufacturers still differentiate on hardware.

### "Why should we help a potential competitor?"
**We're not competing.** We:
- Don't make hardware
- Don't sell content
- Don't run a store
- Are infrastructure (like OpenXR or Vulkan)

We help you compete against other manufacturers by expanding your developer pool.

### "What's in it for us?"
**Three things:**
1. More developers supporting your platform (lower barriers)
2. More content for your users (larger library)
3. Positioning as open/developer-friendly (brand benefits)

All for minimal cost (hardware loan/donation).

### "How do we know this will be maintained?"
**Multiple sustainability paths:**
- Open-source community contributions
- Enterprise support/licensing
- Cloud services
- Training and certification
- Proven open-source model (Linux, Blender, etc.)

## Contingency Plans

### If response rate is low:
- Test different messaging
- Leverage community for social proof
- Build demo first, then request hardware
- Alternative: buy used hardware, request reimbursement later

### If manufacturers request exclusivity:
- Politely decline (conflicts with open mission)
- Offer "launch partner" status (first, not exclusive)
- Find alternative manufacturer

### If budget is needed:
- Prioritize 2-3 most popular headsets
- Community hardware loans
- Phased rollout
