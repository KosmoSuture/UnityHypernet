## Key Decision Points

### Decision 1: Bootstrap vs. Fundraise

**Bootstrap:**
- Pros: Keep equity, maintain control, prove model
- Cons: Slow growth, miss window, can't compete with funded competitors
- Timeline: 18-36 months to reach $1M revenue

**Fundraise:**
- Pros: Fast growth, capture market, hire talent, build moat
- Cons: Dilution, investor pressure, higher expectations
- Timeline: 12-18 months to reach $10M+ revenue

**Recommendation:** Fundraise. The market window is NOW. AI boom won't wait.

### Decision 2: YC vs. Direct to Angels

**Y Combinator:**
- Pros: $500K + network + credibility + Demo Day
- Cons: 7% equity, must relocate to SF for 3 months, rigid timeline
- Best if: You need credibility and network

**Direct to Angels:**
- Pros: Keep more equity, flexible timeline, custom terms
- Cons: Harder to find investors, less structure, more work
- Best if: You have strong network already

**Recommendation:** If you can get 5+ warm intros to top angels, go direct. Otherwise, apply to YC as backup.

### Decision 3: B2B vs. B2C First

**B2B (AI companies):**
- Pros: Faster revenue, larger contracts, strategic partnerships
- Cons: Longer sales cycles, fewer customers, dependency risk

**B2C (consumers):**
- Pros: Viral potential, network effects, brand value
- Cons: Slower monetization, higher CAC, support burden

**Recommendation:** B2B2C - sell to AI companies who drive consumers to you. Best of both.
