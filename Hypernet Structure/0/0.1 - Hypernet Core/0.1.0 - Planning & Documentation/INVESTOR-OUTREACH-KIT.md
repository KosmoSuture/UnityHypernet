## Investor Meeting Checklist

### Before Meeting

**Bring:**
- [ ] Meta Quest 3 (fully charged, backup battery)
- [ ] Laptop (backup slides, demo if needed)
- [ ] Leave-behind deck (printed, 1-2 copies)
- [ ] Business cards
- [ ] Notebook + pen

**Prepare:**
- [ ] Research investor (portfolio, thesis, background)
- [ ] Practice pitch 5+ times that day
- [ ] Test demo (make sure it works!)
- [ ] Anticipate questions

### During Meeting

**Structure (30-45 minutes):**
1. **Small talk** (2 min) - Build rapport
2. **Problem** (3 min) - Make them feel the pain
3. **Solution overview** (5 min) - High-level concept
4. **DEMO** (15 min) - Put them in headset!
5. **Business model** (5 min) - How you make money
6. **Ask** (3 min) - $500K, terms, timeline
7. **Q&A** (10 min) - Answer their questions
8. **Next steps** (2 min) - What happens now?

**Do:**
- ‚úÖ Let them try the demo themselves
- ‚úÖ Start with wow moment (best photo, AI query)
- ‚úÖ Tell stories (emotional > data)
- ‚úÖ Ask for feedback
- ‚úÖ Take notes on their questions

**Don't:**
- ‚ùå Talk too fast (nerves)
- ‚ùå Get defensive about criticism
- ‚ùå Oversell (let demo speak)
- ‚ùå Forget to ask for the investment

**Within 24 hours:**

## Handling Common Objections

### "How do you compete with Google/Meta?"
**Answer:** "They're not building this. Google wants data siloed (it's their business model). Meta's focused on social VR, not personal data management. We're serving an unmet need they can't/won't serve."

### "What's your moat?"
**Answer:** "Three things: 1) Network effects - more user data = better AI = more users. 2) Switching costs - once your life is in Hypernet, hard to leave. 3) Hypernet Addressing system - technical architecture that's genuinely innovative."

### "Why will people pay $10/month?"
**Answer:** "They're already paying for Notion, iCloud, Dropbox, etc. - that's $20-30/month fragmented. Plus, they EARN money from AI marketplace. So net cost might be zero or negative."

### "This seems early for VR?"
**Answer:** "Quest 3 just hit $500 price point with great quality. 10M+ units sold. Apple just validated premium market with Vision Pro. Early, yes - but that's the opportunity. We're building the infrastructure layer before everyone else realizes they need it."

### "How do you get users?"
**Answer:** "Two angles: 1) VR enthusiasts want apps that use their real data. 2) Privacy-conscious people want data control. We're targeting both. Plus viral loop: 'I'm earning $20/month from my data' is extremely shareable."

### "What if regulations change?"
**Answer:** "Regulations are moving IN OUR FAVOR. GDPR, CCPA give users data rights. AI regulations will require transparency. We're building what regulations are demanding."

### "Team is small?"
**Answer:** "Intentionally lean until funded. I've architected everything, built working demo. With $500K, I hire 2 senior engineers who've shipped VR apps. We'll move fast."

## Terms to Aim For

### Seed Round Structure

**Option 1: Priced Round**
- Amount: $500,000
- Valuation: $3M-$5M post-money
- Equity: 10-15%
- Board seat: Negotiable (probably give one)

**Option 2: SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)**
- Amount: $500,000
- Valuation cap: $3M-$5M
- Discount: 20% (standard)
- No board seat yet

**What to Accept:**
- ‚úÖ Standard 4-year vesting with 1-year cliff
- ‚úÖ Pro-rata rights (investors can invest in next round)
- ‚úÖ Board observer (if no board seat)
- ‚úÖ Quarterly updates

**What to Push Back On:**
- ‚ùå Excessive dilution (< 10% is hard to get excited about)
- ‚ùå Super-voting rights
- ‚ùå Blocking rights on future raises
- ‚ùå Personal guarantees

### First Check vs. Lead

**First $100K (First Check):**
- Often easier to close
- Use to create momentum
- Angels or micro VCs
- Less diligence, faster

**Lead Investor ($200K-300K):**
- Sets terms for round
- Does most diligence
- Brings credibility
- Helps close rest

**Strategy:** Get first $100K quickly, use it to attract lead, lead brings rest.
