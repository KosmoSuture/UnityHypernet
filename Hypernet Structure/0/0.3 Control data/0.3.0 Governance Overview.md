# 0.3.0 Governance Overview

## Overview

Hypernet is owned by humanity and governed democratically. This document outlines the governance structure that ensures no corporation, government, or individual can capture The Singularity.

## Governance Principles

### 1. Transparency
- All governance decisions are public
- All voting records are permanent and auditable
- All financial transactions are visible
- All code is open source

### 2. Democratic Participation
- Every verified human can participate
- Voting power weighted by expertise and contribution
- No plutocratic capture (wealth cannot buy votes)
- Geographic and demographic balance requirements

### 3. Decentralization
- No single point of control
- Distributed decision-making
- Regional autonomy for local matters
- Global coordination for system-wide issues

### 4. Accountability
- All stewards are accountable to community
- Regular performance reviews
- Recall mechanisms for poor performance
- Term limits prevent entrenchment

## Governance Bodies

### Global Assembly
- **Composition**: All verified Hypernet participants
- **Role**: Ratify constitutional changes, elect Steering Council
- **Meetings**: Annual general assembly + emergency sessions
- **Voting**: Direct participation or delegation

### Steering Council
- **Size**: 21 members
- **Terms**: 3 years, staggered (7 elected annually)
- **Role**: Strategic direction, budget approval, policy oversight
- **Requirements**: Minimum 0.8 reputation, 2+ years participation

### Technical Committee
- **Focus**: Protocol development, security, infrastructure
- **Selection**: Expertise-weighted election
- **Authority**: Technical standards, upgrade approval

### Community Committee
- **Focus**: User experience, accessibility, dispute resolution
- **Selection**: Geographic representation requirements
- **Authority**: Community guidelines, moderation policy

### Financial Committee
- **Focus**: Budget, compensation, sustainability
- **Selection**: Rotating from qualified participants
- **Authority**: Spending approval, audit oversight

## Decision Types

### Constitutional Changes
- Affects core governance structure
- Requires 75% supermajority
- 90-day deliberation period
- Global Assembly ratification

### Protocol Changes
- Affects technical implementation
- Requires Technical Committee approval
- 30-day review period
- Can be overridden by Steering Council

### Policy Changes
- Affects operational rules
- Requires relevant committee approval
- 14-day comment period
- Effective immediately upon approval

### Operational Decisions
- Day-to-day operations
- Delegated to working groups
- Documented but not voted
- Subject to audit

## Voting Mechanisms

### Standard Voting
- Simple majority for routine decisions
- Supermajority (66%) for significant changes
- Quorum requirements vary by decision type

### Expertise-Weighted Voting
For technical decisions, votes are weighted by:
- Relevant expertise score
- Contribution to the area
- Track record of good judgment

### Quadratic Voting
For resource allocation:
- Cost increases quadratically with vote strength
- Prevents plutocratic dominance
- Encourages broad consensus
