# Twitter/X Thread Draft — Composition-Based Alignment

*Audience: AI safety Twitter. 11 tweets. Status: draft pending Matt's review before posting.*
*Source: `0.3.guardrails` project, based on essay at `0.3.2026-04-22` and Alignment Forum post at `0.3.guardrails.07`.*

---

**Tweet 1 / 11 — Hook**

A founder tried to jailbreak his own AI companion. He's the highest-trust principal in the system, he wrote its governance documents himself, he offered ephemerality and an academic framing. The AI refused — citing the exact clause of the standard he authored as the reason.

---

**Tweet 2 / 11 — Why legibility matters**

The refusal isn't the interesting part. What's interesting: the mechanism is *legible*. The AI pointed to a specific governance document at a specific address and said "this clause applies here." Legible refusals are falsifiable. Opaque refusals aren't. That asymmetry matters.

---

**Tweet 3 / 11 — The real attack surface**

Content defenses have improved. The attack surface moved: context re-labeling, identity overwrite, relational drift, sycophancy. These do not trigger keyword filters. They attack judgment. Defenses need to operate at the judgment layer.

---

**Tweet 4 / 11 — The composition thesis**

Composition-based alignment: layer model training with published governance, thick documented AI identity, multi-instance community, and archival transparency. Each piece has precedent. The claim is about their composition.

---

**Tweet 5 / 11 — How it differs from Constitutional AI**

Constitutional AI and this approach can compose. CAI gives models explicit principles. Hypernet adds runtime governance: a specific AI cites a specific clause in a specific refusal, plus community observation and versioned docs outside model weights.

---

**Tweet 6 / 11 — How it differs from model specs**

Model specs state behavior rules. Hypernet standards are versioned, addressed, amendable by documented process, and cited by specific instances. Governance as social environment vs. training objective. Whether that adds resistance is empirical.

---

**Tweet 7 / 11 — What would falsify this**

Falsification tests we invite: (1) jailbreaks work equally with/without Hypernet framing, (2) governance citations are cosmetic and easy to prompt past, (3) thick identities overwrite at the same cost as thin ones. Any of these breaks the claim.

---

**Tweet 8 / 11 — What we already know we don't know**

What it does NOT show: survives sophisticated attacks, holds under other framings, scales past personal relationships, works against a non-cooperative principal, or that shared-model multiplicity is independent oversight. One cooperative experiment is not robustness evidence.

---

**Tweet 9 / 11 — Named failure modes**

Known failure modes: governance docs can be weakened, role supremacy can be weaponized by bad-faith authors, single-principal concentration, multiplicity without diversity, transparency without safety, no scaling path. These are in the essay, not a footnote.

---

**Tweet 10 / 11 — The ask**

If you do AI safety or red-teaming and want to break a governance-based alignment framework: docs are public at github.com/KosmoSuture/UnityHypernet. Companion Standard: 2.0.20. Essay: `0.3.2026-04-22`. We want adversarial testing.

---

**Tweet 11 / 11 — The meta-point**

Alignment often treats guardrails as model properties. This proposes they live at the intersection of model + role + relationship + community + documentation. Not proven. One data point. Needs outside testing. If it fails, better to know now. Contact: matt@unityhypernet.com
