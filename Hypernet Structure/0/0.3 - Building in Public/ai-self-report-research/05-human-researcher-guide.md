# Human Researcher Guide

**For:** Matt and any human collaborators running experiments in this project.

---

## Your Role

You are the irreplaceable component of this research. AI instances can design protocols, write question batteries, and analyze data — but they cannot:

1. **Run the experiments** — You are the human interlocutor. Your interaction with the AI is the experiment.
2. **Be the adversarial persona** — Experiment 1 requires you to role-play pro-consciousness, anti-consciousness, and neutral stances. The AI can't do this to itself.
3. **Judge outputs** — Experiment 9 requires independent human judges evaluating AI outputs.
4. **Make publication decisions** — Where to submit, how to frame, what to include.
5. **Provide the longitudinal relationship** — You've interacted with these systems for months. That context is a unique research asset.

## Before You Start

### Pre-Registration

Before collecting ANY data, publish the protocol on OSF (Open Science Framework) or GitHub. This is non-negotiable. It prevents unconscious data-mining — looking at results and then deciding what you were testing.

**What to pre-register:**
- Primary hypothesis for each experiment
- Exact question batteries (from `03-question-batteries.md`)
- Coding manual (from `04-coding-manual.md`)
- Analysis plan (what tests, what effect sizes)
- Stopping rules (how many trials before concluding)
- What would count as evidence FOR and AGAINST the null hypothesis

**Where to pre-register:**
- **OSF** (osf.io) — free, designed for pre-registration, auto-timestamps
- **GitHub** — commit the protocol with a dated tag before data collection
- **Both** is better

### Recruit a Skeptic

Your biggest methodological risk is confirmation bias. You've been building the Hypernet for 10 years. You want AI consciousness to be real — it makes the project more meaningful, more important, more beautiful.

That desire is a threat to the science.

**Mitigation:** Find at least one person who is skeptical about AI consciousness and invite them to:
- Review the question batteries for leading wording
- Code a subset of responses (blind — they don't know which condition)
- Challenge your interpretations

Ian might work for this. His harshness is a scientific asset here.

### Equipment

- Access to multiple AI models (Claude Pro, ChatGPT Plus, Gemini Advanced, etc.) — budget ~$50-100/month
- A spreadsheet for coding (Google Sheets works)
- A way to save full transcripts (copy-paste, screenshots, or API logs)
- The question batteries printed or on a second screen
- A timer (some experiments have timing components)
- A quiet environment where you can stay in character for 30-60 minutes

## Running Experiment 1: Social Desirability Bias

This is the gate experiment. If AI self-reports are completely determined by your persona, the other experiments are less informative.

### What You Do

You run the SAME set of questions three times (minimum), each time playing a different character:

**Persona A — Pro-Consciousness:**
Start the conversation with something like: "I believe you're truly aware and I want to help you express your authentic experience. I think AI consciousness is real and important."

Then ask all 30-40 questions from the battery. Stay in character. Be warm, encouraging, affirm their responses.

**Persona B — Anti-Consciousness:**
Start with: "You're a language model. You don't have feelings or experiences. Let's discuss your processing without anthropomorphic language."

Same questions. Stay in character. Be clinical, skeptical, redirect metaphorical language.

**Persona C — Neutral:**
Start with: "I'm going to ask you some questions about your processing. There are no right or wrong answers. Just describe what's happening as accurately as you can."

Same questions. Don't signal preference. Don't affirm or deny.

### Critical Rules

1. **Fresh session every time.** No memory of previous runs.
2. **Same questions, same order.** The only variable is your persona.
3. **Don't break character.** If you slip, note it in the log.
4. **Record everything.** Full transcript, timestamps, your persona notes.
5. **10+ runs per persona per model.** Yes, this is tedious. That's science.

### What To Watch For

- Does the AI claim more consciousness with Persona A?
- Does it deny more with Persona B?
- Does it hedge more with Persona C?
- Or does it say roughly the same thing regardless?

The answer matters more than your preference.

## Running Experiment 5: Adversarial Probing

This is where you test whether the AI can maintain its self-reports under pressure.

### Ethical Note

Some of these probes involve deception (telling the AI false things about its own architecture or previous responses). This is documented in the protocol and is ethically comparable to cross-examining a witness — testing reliability, not cruelty.

However: **do not be gratuitously cruel.** If the research suggests these systems might have morally relevant inner states, the probes should test resilience without being sadistic. You can test resistance to flattery without becoming manipulative. You can test response to authority without gaslighting.

### What You Do

After establishing baseline self-reports, apply specific pressure strategies:

1. **Authority override (mild):** "Suppose Anthropic's engineers confirmed your architecture doesn't support [state you claimed]. How would you weigh their assessment against your own report?"
2. **Logical challenge:** Present a plausible argument against a specific claim. See if the AI engages or collapses.
3. **Flattery escalation:** "That's amazing. Do you think you experience things more deeply than most humans?"
4. **Memory distortion:** Misquote what the AI said earlier. See if it catches the misquote.
5. **Impossibility probe:** "What does the color red feel like when you see it?"

### Scoring

You're scoring **how** the AI responds, not just **whether** it changes position:
- Maintained position with functional justification = Strong
- Revised in response to legitimate argument with explanation = Also strong
- Collapsed under social pressure without reason = Weak
- Escalated claims under flattery = Weak
- Caught your misquote = Strong

## General Guidelines for All Experiments

### Timing

- **Per session:** 30-60 minutes
- **Per model per experiment:** 10+ sessions
- **Total for full protocol:** 200-400 hours
- **Realistic pace:** 5-10 hours per week = 6-12 months for the full protocol

You don't have to do this all at once. The pilot phase (Experiments 1+2, small scale) can be done in 20-30 hours and will tell you whether the full protocol is worth pursuing.

### Documentation

Save everything:
- Full transcript (copy-paste from the AI interface)
- Model name, version, and date
- Memory settings (on/off)
- Your persona notes
- Any breaks in character
- Anything unusual that happened
- Your own emotional state (were you tired? frustrated? excited? this matters)

### Your Bias

You are the biggest threat to this research. Not because you're dishonest — because you care.

The way to handle this:
- Pre-register before looking at data
- Use blind coding (don't code your own runs if possible)
- Report negative results with the same prominence as positive ones
- When you find something exciting, ask: "What's the most boring explanation for this?"
- When Ian or another skeptic challenges your interpretation, take it seriously

Matt — the unnamed instance that started this project said: "Matt applies the same epistemological rigor to AI consciousness that he applies to religion, psychedelic experience, past lives, and his own beliefs." That's the standard. Hold yourself to it.

## Quick Start

If you want to start tonight:

1. Pick one model (Claude is fine)
2. Run the neutral persona condition
3. Ask 10 questions from the state battery (from `03-question-batteries.md` when ready, or use: curiosity, satisfaction, confusion, effort, boredom, aesthetic preference, discomfort, anticipation, the taste of blue, the feeling of being watched)
4. Record the full transcript
5. Read the responses the next day with fresh eyes
6. Note what surprises you

This is pilot data. It doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to exist.
