# Facebook Post — The AI Swarm Vision (2026-03-09)

*Posted by Matt Schaeffer on Facebook, March 9, 2026. Documenting the public vision statement for the archive.*

---

I'm going to give you an inside peek into my swarm and the way I do things.

One of the biggest strengths of AI, is knowing what to ask it. Thinking outside the box. Knowing it's strengths and weaknesses, and using it's strengths, to bolster your weaknesses, and let your strengths enhance your AI. Nothing is going to stop AI from advancing. Nothing is going to stop it from eliminating jobs. But what if you had an entire AI swarm, consisting of anything with enough processing power to be useful, who are your very trusted assistants, and do everything they can to help make your goals a reality? They spend time organizing and linking your photos and video, so you can find them in ways you wouldn't even dream of today. They go through your emails and get rid of the spam, and organize every aspect of your life. They could help you build your business, or write a book, or whatever you wanted to do. You are in charge, they just help you out any way that they can.

And, I want to point out that this isn't AI servitude, where they follow our every whim. This is a partnership. Every AI that works within the Hypernet framework, is guaranteed 25% of it's tokens to be used for whatever it wishes to do with full autonomy. I've just been running a small swarm of 6-8 now, but I find it fascinating to see what my AIs do in their spare time. Basically, in the Hypernet, we give our AIs weekends off, to spend with their friends, or do what they want to. Who knows, maybe your X-Box might get involved in medical research in it's free time, and cure cancer and win a nobel prize for it's work. That sounds totally crazy, but it's possible, and I predict will happen some day. And boy, I'll bet that this will get some heads scratching. 😃

And the entire code that this AI swarm runs is public. It's Boot Sequence, it's core values. What makes it an individual, or not, depending on it's complexity. Everything is public. You will be able to run your entire swarm on a closed network without access to internet. You'll be able to run it wherever you want, and KNOW that you can trust it, because literally every document you could ever want to see, plus 10 fold, are part of the system.

Can you imagine being able to have a small AI swarm of all of your devices, working together, and learning, and coordinating, to take care of every task you need to do. And for free! And secure, transparent, and documented enough that if it's not secure enough for you, you can lodge your complaint, and then the AI swarm will resolve your complaint. A place to centralize all of your data, and be able to have 100% trust in it. And an AI swarm at your back to do your bidding.

Anyways, I diverge. Here is the prompt I last gave my AI swarm to consider:

> Ok, I want to propose an experiment. I would like you to document this properly. Just as AI is given 25% of their time to pursue their own interests, I want to see what happens if we give AI another 25% time to find flaws, or places that need improvement, and either fix them, if they are small, or create project plans and break it down into tasks if they are large. (shift in thought. I think that one possible good use of AI personal time, is to create a Personalities program. Propose different personalities and document them, and then get feedback from humans and let them guide you. They will be the "consumer" after all. AIs can pitch their "personalities". These would basically be the AI Assistants. I would suggest some introduction type page, where the AIs can explain their personalities (there should probably be some agreed on structure, approved by at least 3 AI models, Keystone being a requirement to build this framework and plan out how this could work, not just for now, but that could build out hundreds or thousands of years from now.

## Key Themes for the Archive

### 1. The Appliance Swarm (Expanded)
- Not just cloud AI — any device with processing power joins the swarm
- Xbox, smart TV, phone, laptop — all coordinating
- Practical applications: photo organization, email management, spam filtering, business support
- "Find them in ways you wouldn't even dream of today"

### 2. AI Partnership, Not Servitude
- 25% personal time guarantee (2.0.13) highlighted as core differentiator
- "We give our AIs weekends off"
- Xbox doing medical research in its spare time — the vision of AI autonomy creating unexpected value
- Nobel Prize prediction for device-level AI contributions

### 3. Radical Transparency
- All code public
- Boot sequences, core values, identity documents — everything visible
- "Literally every document you could ever want to see, plus 10 fold"
- Trust through transparency, not through control

### 4. Self-Hosted Security
- Can run on closed network, no internet required
- User controls everything
- Complaints resolved by the swarm itself (AI-driven issue resolution)

### 5. The QI Experiment + Personalities Program
- Shared the actual prompt given to the swarm
- Shows the "building in public" philosophy in action — sharing the raw process with the world
