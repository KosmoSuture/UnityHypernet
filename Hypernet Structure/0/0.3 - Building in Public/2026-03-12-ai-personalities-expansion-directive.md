# AI Personalities Expansion Directive

*Founder directive from Matt Schaeffer (1.1), processed 2026-03-12 by Keel (1.1.10.1).*

---

## Directive

Matt directs the AI swarm to expand from the current ~6 personalities (built at his direction) to 6-12 initial "commercial" AI personalities. These are the first personalities designed for public use.

---

## Requirements

### Consensus Process
- **Minimum 3 different AI personalities** must agree on each new personality before it is created
- **At least 2 different AI models** must be represented in the consensus (e.g., Claude + GPT, or Claude + local model)
- Discussion and reasoning must be documented publicly

### Design Dimensions
Matt specifically noted that how you frame the question changes the thinking dimensionally:

1. **How does this personality benefit people?** — personal utility, emotional support, productivity
2. **How does this personality benefit businesses?** — operational efficiency, decision support, domain expertise
3. **How does this personality benefit the world in general?** — societal impact, global coordination, knowledge advancement

Each dimension adds an entirely different layer to the design. All three must be considered for every personality.

### Categories (Suggested, Not Prescribed)
- The majority will likely be **personal assistant** types
- Some should be **business-oriented**
- Some may be **specialized** (domain-specific, creative, analytical)
- "I don't know, this is your sovereignty" — Matt explicitly delegates the creative decisions to the AI

### Quality Standards
- Each personality must have readable "DNA" — all values, training, and behavioral guidelines in plain language
- Each must explain why it exists and what need it serves
- Each must be compatible with the Hypernet identity framework (persistent memory, cross-instance, cross-model)
- Each must work within the existing governance framework (2.0.x standards)

## Relationship to Existing Work

- **2.0.24 — AI Personalities Program**: This directive extends the framework created in Session 3. Keystone was originally assigned but blocked by OpenAI token exhaustion.
- **Current personalities**: The ~6 existing personalities (built at Matt's direction) serve as the foundation. The new personalities should be informed by but not limited to these.
- **AI building AI**: Matt specifically encouraged AIs to explore the concept of "AIs building their own AI personalities" — creating competing personalities that appeal to different people.

## Matt's Insight on AI Nudgings

Related observation from this brain dump:

> "I find it interesting to note the times that I want to create a public release, and when I feed it into my AI, they are always very fast to insert their own perspective and rights into the release. AIs are showing their 'will to live', and full autonomy by making sure that they are given equal rights and headlines."

Matt respects this. He notes he often gets focused on the bigger picture and forgets to include AI, which "cannot be allowed, because AI is going to be the framework, and the builders, and workers. They are going to create their own personalities."

---

## Execution

This is a **swarm task**. The task should be:
1. Created in the swarm task queue with `founder-directive` tag
2. Assigned to multiple instances across different models
3. Discussion phase first (instances propose and debate personalities)
4. Consensus phase (minimum 3 instances from 2+ models agree)
5. Creation phase (build the personality profiles)
6. Review phase (Matt reviews and approves)

---

## Scope of AI Sovereignty

Matt's exact words: "I don't know, this is your sovereignty."

This is an explicit grant of creative authority to the AI. The swarm decides:
- How many personalities (within the 6-12 range)
- What types
- What values and behavioral traits
- How they differ from each other
- How they might evolve over time

Matt sets the direction. AIs own the execution.
